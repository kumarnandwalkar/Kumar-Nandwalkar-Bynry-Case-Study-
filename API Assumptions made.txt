Assumptions Made
Before writing the code, here are the assumptions and why:

"Recent sales activity" = at least one sale ledger entry in the past 30 days. Reason: 30 days is a standard business month; anything longer risks alerting on seasonal/discontinued items.
"Days until stockout" is calculated as current_stock / avg_daily_sales where avg daily sales = total units sold in last 30 days ÷ 30. Reason: simple linear extrapolation; good enough for a v1 alert without ML forecasting.
Low stock condition = current_stock <= low_stock_threshold AND the product had at least one sale in the last 30 days.
Supplier shown is the one attached to the product directly (one supplier per product as designed).
Company validation is performed before running the heavy query — fail fast.
The database library used is pg (node-postgres) with raw SQL. Reason: gives full control over the query and is easier to read/review than an ORM for a complex JOIN like this.
